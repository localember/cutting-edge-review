The short answer
Pediatric chiropractic care is gentle, low-force care for a child's spine and posture as they grow. The focus is on comfort and movement: easing the aches and stiffness that active, growing, screen-using kids pick up, using a fraction of the force used with an adult. It is careful, unhurried, and honest about what it is and is not for.
Parents ask good, careful questions about chiropractic care for kids, and they should. Here is a straight look at what the care actually is, what it is for, what a visit looks like with your child, and where we draw the line honestly.

What is pediatric chiropractic care?

It is gentle care for a growing musculoskeletal system. Kids are not small adults; their joints and muscles are still developing, so the care is far lighter and always matched to the child in front of us. The aim is comfort and easy movement: helping a stiff, achy, or guarded area settle so a child can move and play without it nagging.
We want to be plain about scope. Pediatric chiropractic care is about the musculoskeletal side, posture, movement, and the aches that come with growing and active bodies. It is not a substitute for your pediatrician, and it is not care for infections, illnesses, or developmental conditions. If your child needs a doctor, that is where we will send you.

What can it help with?

The honest list is a musculoskeletal one, and it grows with the child:
  • The posture and neck-and-shoulder stiffness that come with school bags and screens.
  • Aches and niggles from sport, growth spurts, and active play.
  • Stiffness lingering after a fall, a sprain, or an awkward landing.
  • Older kids and teens with the same desk-and-phone strain adults get, earlier.
What we will not do is claim chiropractic care helps with colic, ear infections, or behavioural and developmental conditions. The evidence does not support those claims, and we will not make them. Where a concern is medical, your pediatrician leads and we say so.

What is a visit like for a child?

Unhurried, and with you in the room the whole time. It starts as a conversation, with you and, depending on age, with your child. The check and the care are gentle enough that the pressure used on a small child is closer to what you would use to check a ripe tomato than anything forceful. Nothing happens until your child is comfortable, and you are welcome to ask us to stop at any point.

What does care cost, and do you take insurance?

No referral is needed to book, and we are happy to check your benefits for you before the first visit so there are no surprises. Costs depend on your plan, and we will always walk you through it in plain numbers first.
  • No referral needed to book a first visit.
  • We verify your benefits for you ahead of time, so you know what to expect.
  • Clear, up-front pricing if you are paying out of pocket, with no packages you did not ask for.
We cannot promise a specific outcome or what your insurance will cover. Pediatric chiropractic care is a complement to your child's medical care, never a replacement for their pediatrician. Anything that needs a doctor, we will point you to first.

Aches and back complaints are far more common in kids than parents expect, and they are worth taking seriously rather than waving off as growing pains.
