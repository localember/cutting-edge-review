The short answer
Acupuncture is the placing of very thin, sterile, single-use needles at specific points on the body. In our office it is used as one option among several, alongside chiropractic care, mostly for muscle tension and some kinds of ongoing pain. It suits some people well and is not the answer for everyone, and the honest version of that sentence matters more than a bigger claim.
Acupuncture attracts strong opinions in both directions. Here is the grounded version: what it is, what a session is actually like, where we find it useful, and where the evidence is honestly mixed.

What is acupuncture?

Fine needles, much thinner than the ones used for injections, are placed at particular points and left in place for a short while. Most people feel little more than a small pinch or nothing at all, and many find the session itself calming. The needles are single-use and sterile, opened in front of you.
We use it as part of a wider plan rather than as a standalone fix, matched to what your body is telling us during the examination.

Who is it for?

We tend to reach for it when muscle tension and certain ongoing aches are part of the picture and someone would rather try a low-force, drug-free option.
  • Tight, ropey muscle that keeps returning after it is worked.
  • Ongoing neck or low back ache that has not settled with other care alone.
  • People who want to avoid or reduce medication where they reasonably can.
  • Tension headaches tied to a stiff, guarded neck and shoulders.
It is not right for everyone, and there are situations where we would not use it or would refer you elsewhere first. That is part of what the first visit is for.

Is acupuncture proven to work?

Honestly, the evidence is mixed and depends heavily on what you are asking it to do. For some kinds of ongoing pain it appears to help a meaningful number of people; for others the picture is far less clear. We offer it as an option worth trying for the right person, not as a guarantee, and we will tell you plainly if we do not think it is your best next step.

What does a session involve?

You rest comfortably while the needles are placed, then stay still for a short period, often the most relaxing part of someone's week. Afterwards you carry on with your day. We usually pair it with the hands-on care and movement that address why the area got overloaded in the first place.

What does it cost, and do you take insurance?

Coverage for acupuncture varies widely by plan, and many do not cover it at all. We are happy to check your benefits for you first and give you clear numbers before anything begins.
  • No referral needed to book a first visit.
  • We verify your benefits for you ahead of time, so you know what to expect.
  • Clear, up-front pricing if you are paying out of pocket, with no packages you did not ask for.
We cannot promise a specific outcome or what your insurance will cover, and acupuncture is not right for every case. What we can promise is a sterile, careful approach, an honest read on whether it fits, and a straight answer either way.
